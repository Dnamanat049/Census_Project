Georgia

Did the U.S. become more diverse between 2000 and 2010?

Key: Dark Orange is the national average, and light yellow is the state average

Georgia had a 8.824925% increase in WHITE inhabitants between 2000 and 2010. This was less than the national increase in population of ALL RACES of 9.7%, which means that Georgia likely became more diverse!

The state had 2070172 white inhabitants in 2000 and had 2270546 white inhabitants in 2010.
