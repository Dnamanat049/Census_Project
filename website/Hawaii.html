Did the U.S. become more diverse between 2000 and 2010?

Key: Dark Orange is the national average, and light yellow is the state average

Hawaii had a 15.479856% increase in WHITE inhabitants between 2000 and 2010. This was greater than the national increase in population of ALL RACES of 9.7%, which means that Hawaii likely became less diverse.

The state had 127382 white inhabitants in 2000 and had 150712 white inhabitants in 2010.
