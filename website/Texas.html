Texas

Did the U.S. become more diverse between 2000 and 2010?

Key: Dark Orange is the national average, and light yellow is the state average

Texas had a 15.579441% increase in WHITE inhabitants between 2000 and 2010. This was greater than the national increase in population of ALL RACES of 9.7%, which means that Texas likely became less diverse.

The state had 5546559 white inhabitants in 2000 and had 6570152 white inhabitants in 2010.
