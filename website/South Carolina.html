South Carolina

Did the U.S. become more diverse between 2000 and 2010?

Key: Dark Orange is the national average, and light yellow is the state average

South Carolina had a 13.370596% increase in WHITE inhabitants between 2000 and 2010. This was greater than the national increase in population of ALL RACES of 9.7%, which means that South Carolina likely became less diverse.

The state had 1089428 white inhabitants in 2000 and had 1257573 white inhabitants in 2010.
